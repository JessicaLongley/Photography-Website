Photography Basics

The photo cheat sheet above helped me start to discover the full potential of my Nikon D3100.
This three week course has chipped away at six years of automatic settings. I have learned that Aperature lets in different amounts of light depending on its size. A camera's Shutter Speed controls how fast the photo is taken. ISO is the sensor sensitivity of the camera. Photography is all about managing and compromising aperature, shutter speed, and ISO.

Portfolio

I have taken a variety of types of photos for this class. We started with portraits and ended with a very open-ended deadly sin project. Here are some of my best photos and what I learned as I took them.

Good landscapes have a foreground, middle ground, and background.

Most good photos require tack sharpness. Also, select by color is a useful feature in Photoshop.

The elements of design apply to photographs. Sometimes it helps to think about the picture in an abstract way when editing.

The way that you talk about your piece will influence how people see it.

Focus on a person's best features. It helps to keep both eyes in focus.

In this photo the grass is less interesting than the sky, so less grass is included.

Sustained interest is necessary. A simple how to is boring.

Stay eye level and use facial expressions to show emotion. Often the eyes carry the piece.

Use a longer lens to shoot wildlife.

A faster shutter speed will darken a photo.

RAW photos have more options for editing.

We all want to be understood and accepted. Be a messenger to tell stories.

Smaller aperatures will blur backgrounds. This is best partnered with a sharp focal point.

Horizon rules can be broken.

Use Photoshop's dodge and burn tools to manipulate a photo's brightest and darkest areas.

Hold very still or used a stand when shooting with a lower shutter speed.

Photoshop's selection tool is versitile. Dithering gradients will reduce the number of visible lines.

Objects in the foreground of a landscape have the potential to help or hurt the piece.

Compositions have the ability to guide a viewer's eye through a piece in a specific path.

Time of day can make all the diference in landscape photography. The best times to shoot are around sunrise and sunset.
